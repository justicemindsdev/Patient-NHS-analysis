Causation Report: White Ferry Targeting → Mr. Gany Crash

Cascading Harms Analysis and Evidence Documentation

Executive Summary

This report documents the direct causal chain connecting targeted harassment at White Ferry to the subsequent vehicle crash involving Mr. Said Gany. A clear timeline of events demonstrates how the targeted behavior at White Ferry caused psychological distress, logistical disruption, and care coordination failure that directly led to Mr. Gany driving while medically unfit, resulting in a vehicle crash that caused significant damages.

The evidence establishes a clear and direct causation chain from White Ferry's targeting behavior to Mr. Gany's vehicle crash through the following sequence:

  1. White Ferry staff targeting → Ben unable to assist with care duties
  2. Care assistance disruption → Mr. Gany forced to self-transport despite dangerous potassium levels (6.2 mmol/L)
  3. Medically unsafe self-transport → Vehicle crash → Total loss of vehicle (VW Polo SX08 CWR)

I. Detailed Causation Analysis

Legal Causation Framework

This report applies the two-stage test of causation established in UK law:

  1. Factual causation ("but for" test): But for the White Ferry targeting, Mr. Gany would not have been forced to transport himself in a medically unsafe condition, and the crash would not have occurred.
  2. Legal causation (reasonable foreseeability): It was reasonably foreseeable that targeting behavior causing distress and disrupting care duties would result in unsafe alternative arrangements and potential harm.

A. White Ferry Targeting - Initial Trigger Event

Evidence of Targeting

White Ferry staff engaged in targeted harassment against Ben, who was providing support services to Mr. Gany's family. This targeting included:

  • Barring Ben without clear explanation despite his requests for reasonable accommodation
  • Ignoring disability accommodation requests (autism)
  • Discriminatory treatment compared to other guests

Source: Recorded conversations with White Ferry staff

B. Disruption of Care Services - Intermediate Cause

Due to the targeting at White Ferry, Ben was:

  • Forced to defend himself from the targeting
  • Unable to fulfill his care duties to cook for Mr. Gany at his home
  • Prevented from providing transportation assistance to Mr. Gany

This resulted in Mr. Gany lacking critical support services that would have prevented the need for self-transport.

C. Mr. Gany's Medical Condition - Vulnerability Factor

Critical Medical Evidence

At the time of the incident, Mr. Gany was experiencing:

  • Dangerously elevated potassium levels of 6.2 mmol/L (normal range: 3.5-5.0 mmol/L)
  • Symptoms including dropping objects from hands (neuromuscular effects)
  • Recent bereavement (loss of Mrs. Gany)
  • Multiple chronic conditions including diabetes, COPD, osteoporosis, and heart disease

These conditions rendered Mr. Gany medically unfit to drive and required professional assistance.

Source: GP referral documentation, potassium test results

D. Vehicle Crash - Ultimate Harm Event

Crash Details

Without Ben's assistance due to the White Ferry targeting, Mr. Gany attempted to drive himself while in a medically compromised state, resulting in:

  • Vehicle crash with his Volkswagen Polo (SX08 CWR)
  • Total loss of the vehicle (scrap value: £166)
  • Property damage at crash location
  • Potential exacerbation of existing medical conditions

Source: Car scrap documentation, crash site photographs

IV. Legal Liability Analysis

A. White Ferry's Liability

Primary Basis of Liability:

  1. Discriminatory Treatment: White Ferry's targeting of Ben despite his disclosed disability (autism) violated the Equality Act 2010
  2. Foreseeable Harm: White Ferry knew or should have known that targeting Ben would disrupt his ability to provide care services to vulnerable individuals
  3. Chain of Causation: Direct and unbroken causal chain from targeting → care disruption → unsafe driving → crash

B. Relevant Legal Precedents

Applicable Case Law:

  • Page v Smith [1996] AC 155: Established that harm need not be of exactly the same type as long as some harm was foreseeable. White Ferry could foresee that targeting would cause some harm, even if not specifically a car crash.
  • Hughes v Lord Advocate [1963] AC 837: "You must take your victim as you find them" - White Ferry must take Ben as they found him, including his responsibilities to vulnerable people.
  • Darnley v Croydon Health Services NHS Trust [2018] UKSC 50: Established that failure to provide accurate information that leads to harm creates liability. White Ferry's failure to explain the targeting contributed to the chain of events.

C. Causation Assessment

Direct and Substantial Causation:

The evidence demonstrates both factual and legal causation between White Ferry's actions and Mr. Gany's crash:

  • But for White Ferry's targeting, Ben would have provided care and transportation assistance
  • But for the lack of care assistance, Mr. Gany would not have needed to drive himself
  • But for driving while medically compromised, the crash would not have occurred

This causal chain is unbroken and direct, establishing White Ferry's liability for the resulting damages.
